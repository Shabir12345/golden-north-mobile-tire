# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

GTA drivers in a moment of friction — a flat or seasonal tire, a dead battery, a
roadside breakdown — who need help to come to *them* rather than driving to a shop.
The defining context is stress and immediacy: many arrive stranded, outdoors, on a
phone, sometimes late at night or in bad weather. A second audience is the planner
booking a seasonal on/off-rim swap or buying tires, who is calmer but still values
"we come to you" convenience.

The job to be done: *get qualified help moving toward me, fast.* The site's success
is measured by the phone call placed, not time on page.

## Product Purpose

The marketing site for **GoldenNorth Mobile Tire Services**, a 24/7 mobile tire and
roadside business serving the Greater Toronto Area (mobile tire changes, new & used
tire sales, battery replacement, roadside assistance). It exists to turn a stranded
or shopping visitor into a phone call, and to rank for local intent searches
("mobile tire change Toronto", "24/7 roadside tire Scarborough"). Replaces the legacy
goldennorthmobiletires.com with a stronger, distinctive, SEO-solid rebuild.

Success looks like: a visitor on a phone at the side of the road understands within
one screen that GoldenNorth will come to them now, and taps **Call (416) 558-5915**.

## Brand Personality

**Friendly · dependable · clear.** A helpful local service brand. Direct, plain
language ("We come to you"), calm and reassuring. The identity is the
**Clean & Trustworthy** system (white canvas, one trust-blue accent, Inter type):
approachable and easy to navigate.

Emotional goal: calm confidence — *help is on the way, and it's easy to reach.*

## Anti-references

The site must NOT look like any of these:

- **Generic auto-shop sites** — cluttered red/yellow, clip-art tires, stock-photo
  banners, coupon spam.
- **Cheap AI / SaaS templates** — centered hero, a tiny uppercase eyebrow above every
  section, identical icon-cards, cream/sand background, gradient text.
- **Corporate fleet / insurance** — cold, faceless big-company blue with no
  personality.
- **Loud discount-tire retailers** — big-box promo energy, sale tags, price-screaming
  banners.

The tension to manage: convey *24/7 dependability* through the calm Clean & Trustworthy
system (white canvas, one trust-blue accent, Inter), not by importing the visual
language of the loud-retailer or generic-auto-shop lanes we're rejecting. Reassuring ≠
bland; trustworthy ≠ loud-and-cheap.

## Design Principles

1. **Call-first, always.** The phone call is the single conversion action. The trust-blue
   Call button is the dominant CTA on every surface; forms and "contact" links are
   styled as secondary fallbacks that never compete with it.
2. **Calm confidence, not alarm.** Convey 24/7 dependability through clear hierarchy,
   plain operational copy, and a steady single accent — not red/yellow promo noise or
   emergency motifs. The feeling to leave is relief: help is on the way, and it's easy
   to reach.
3. **Operational voice, not marketing voice.** Declarative dispatch language. State the
   uncommon promise plainly; trust it instead of padding with superlatives.
4. **Distinctive over safe.** Commit to the Clean & Trustworthy identity — white canvas,
   one trust-blue accent, Inter, soft cards, the north-star mark. Reject category slop
   and template defaults (cream/sand backgrounds, per-section eyebrows, identical
   icon-cards); calm is the differentiator, not blandness.
5. **Stranded-on-mobile-first.** Design for the worst-case visitor — stressed, outdoors,
   on a phone, possibly at night. Large tap targets, instant clarity, fast loads, the
   call action always within reach.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**: body contrast ≥4.5:1 and large text ≥3:1 (trust-blue-on-white
verified), visible focus states on all interactive elements, semantic landmarks and
heading hierarchy, descriptive alt text on all imagery, and `prefers-reduced-motion`
alternatives for every animation (the reduced-motion block in `globals.css` is
load-bearing — do not remove it). Given the stranded-mobile context, keep tap targets
generous and the primary call action reachable without precise aim.
